Tompkins Math Contest

About the Contest

The Tompkins Math Contest (TMC) prepares middle school students for advanced high school math by using a mix of TMSCA, AMC 8, AMC 10, and AIME problems, totaling 50 questions. The contest features tiebreakers with AIME level questions.


The contest is meant to challenge the critical thinking skills of Junior High students, while also testing their abilites in applying the math formulas they have learned in their previous math classes.

Why Participate

Participating in this contest is an excellent way for junior high students to challenge themselves mathematically and prepare for advanced high school coursework. High scores and awards earned in this competition can significantly enhance a student's academic profile and create opportunities for future recognition. This contest gives students the ability to improve their problem-solving skills while competing against peers and gaining resume-worthy achievements.
